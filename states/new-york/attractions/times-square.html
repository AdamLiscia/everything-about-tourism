Times Square

Dubbed "The Crossroads of the World," Times Square is a bustling commercial and tourist hub in Midtown Manhattan, spanning from West 42nd to West 47th Streets along Broadway and Seventh Avenue. Famous for its towering digital billboards, 39 Broadway theaters, and the annual New Year's Eve ball drop, it attracts over 50 million visitors yearly. This iconic destination pulses with energy 24/7, offering entertainment, dining, and shopping in the heart of NYC's Theater District.

Must-See Attractions

TKTS Booth

Score 20-50% off same-day Broadway and Off-Broadway tickets at this iconic booth under the red steps—a perfect perch for photos and people-watching.

Show Times

Broadway Shows

Catch a world-class performance at one of 41 historic theaters (39 in Times Square area). From musicals like *The Lion King* to dramas, there’s something for all.

Current Shows

Madame Tussauds

Pose with lifelike wax figures of celebrities, from Taylor Swift to Marvel superheroes, in this interactive museum on 42nd Street.

Things to Do

Shopping

Browse flagship stores like the Disney Store, Forever 21, and M&M's World, plus unique NYC souvenirs at dozens of shops.

Dining

Enjoy everything from quick bites at Shake Shack to themed dining at Hard Rock Cafe, Bubba Gump Shrimp Co., or upscale spots like Carmine’s.

Entertainment

Watch street performers, catch live music, or join the iconic New Year's Eve ball drop (plan ahead—crowds exceed 1 million!).

Visitor Information

Getting There

Take the 1, 2, 3, 7, N, Q, R, W, or S subway lines to Times Square-42nd St. Walk from Penn Station (10 min) or Port Authority (5 min). Taxis and rideshares plentiful.

Best Times to Visit

Nighttime dazzles with lights (peak crowds 6-11 PM). Early mornings (before 10 AM) are quieter. Avoid holidays like New Year’s Eve unless joining festivities.

Safety Tips

Heavily policed with NYPD presence (substation at 1560 Broadway). Stay alert for pickpockets. Avoid unofficial ticket sellers—use trusted sources only.

Plan Your Visit

Costs & Accessibility

Free to explore; no entry fee. Broadway tickets range $50-$200+ (discounts at TKTS). Area is wheelchair-accessible; elevators at Times Square-42nd St station.

Visitor Resources

Visit the Times Square Alliance at timessquarenyc.org for events, maps, and live webcams.
