Statue of Liberty National Monument

Standing proudly in New York Harbor, the Statue of Liberty has welcomed millions to America since its dedication on October 28, 1886. A gift from France to celebrate the centennial of American independence, this 305-foot (including pedestal) copper statue symbolizes freedom and democracy. Visitors can explore Liberty Island, the museum, and, with advance booking, ascend to the pedestal or crown for breathtaking views.

Highlights

Statue of Liberty Museum

Opened in 2019, this museum features interactive exhibits on the statue’s history, construction, and legacy, including the original torch.

Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration

Included with your ferry ticket, explore the stories of over 12 million immigrants. Family history research available.

Audio Tour

Complimentary audio guides in 12 languages detail the statue’s history, architecture, and immigration significance.

Visitor Information

Ferry Service

Operated by Statue Cruises, ferries depart from Battery Park (NYC) and Liberty State Park (NJ). Arrive 30-60 minutes early for security screening, similar to airport protocols.

Best Times to Visit

Early morning (first ferry) or late afternoon for smaller crowds. Spring (April-May) and fall (Sept-Oct) offer pleasant weather. Crown tickets require 3-6 months advance booking.

What to Bring

Photo ID (for crown access), comfortable shoes, water, and snacks. Large bags, backpacks, and weapons prohibited; lockers available for small fee.
