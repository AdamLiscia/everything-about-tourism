Empire State Plaza

The Empire State Plaza is a monumental modernist complex housing New York State government offices and cultural institutions. Built between 1965 and 1976, it features striking architecture, including The Egg performing arts center, the New York State Museum, and a world-class art collection displayed throughout its public spaces.

Plaza Highlights

The Egg

Iconic performing arts venue hosting concerts, theater, and dance performances in two distinct theaters.

Event Schedule

NYS Museum

Explore New York's natural and cultural history through engaging exhibits and collections.

Museum Info

Observation Deck

42nd floor viewing area offering panoramic views of Albany, the Hudson Valley, and Adirondacks.

Visit Info

Seasonal Activities

Summer at the Plaza

Free concerts, festivals, and farmers markets. Wednesday night concerts and food vendor series from June through September.

Winter Activities

Ice skating rink with rentals available. Holiday tree lighting and winter festivals. Indoor concourse provides weather protection.

Art Collection

Year-round access to one of the largest public art collections, featuring modern American art throughout the plaza.

Visitor Information

Hours & Access

Plaza open daily. Museum: Tue-Sun 9:30-5. Observation Deck: Mon-Fri 10-4. ID required for building entry.

Parking

Visitor parking available in V-Lot and underground garages. Free weekends and after 6 PM weekdays. Metered street parking nearby.

Local Tips

Use the climate-controlled concourse during extreme weather. Check event calendar for free activities. Tours available through the Plaza Visitor Center.
