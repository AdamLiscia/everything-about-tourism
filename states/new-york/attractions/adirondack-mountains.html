Adirondack Mountains

Welcome to the Adirondacks, a six-million-acre natural wonderland of pristine wilderness, crystal-clear lakes, and soaring mountain peaks. Home to 46 High Peaks over 4,000 feet, this protected park offers year-round outdoor adventures from hiking and camping to world-class skiing.

Must-Do Experiences

High Peaks Hiking

Challenge yourself to become a "46er" by climbing all 46 High Peaks. Mount Marcy, the highest point in NY, offers breathtaking views.

Trail Maps

Lake Placid Olympic Sites

Visit the historic Olympic venues, ride the bobsled track, or ski Whiteface Mountain. Home of the 1932 and 1980 Winter Olympics.

Olympic Site Passport: $35

Learn More

Lake Adventures

Paddle, fish, or swim in over 3,000 lakes and ponds. Explore the Saranac Lake Chain or Lake George, the "Queen of American Lakes."

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

Summer (June-August) for hiking and water activities. Fall for spectacular foliage. Winter (December-March) for skiing and winter sports. Spring for waterfalls and wildflowers.

Getting Around

Car recommended for exploring. Nearest airports: Albany, Burlington (VT), and regional airports. Shuttle services available in Lake Placid area.

Essential Tips

Follow Leave No Trace principles. Carry proper gear for hiking. Check weather conditions. Reserve campsites in advance during peak season.
