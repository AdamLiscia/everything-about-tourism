Jewish Deli

Since 1888, Katz's Delicatessen has been the undisputed king of pastrami in New York City. This iconic Jewish deli is famous for its hand-carved pastrami on rye, hot dogs, matzo ball soup, and the memorable scene from "When Harry Met Sally."

Famous Dishes

Hand-carved, perfectly spiced pastrami served on fresh rye bread. Their most iconic sandwich.

Visitor Tips

Ordering System

Get a ticket when entering. Don't lose it! Order at the counter and tip your cutter. Pay at the register when leaving.

Best Times to Visit

Avoid peak lunch hours (12-2 PM) for shorter lines. Early morning or late evening visits recommended. Expect longer waits on weekends.

Local Tips

Sandwiches are huge - consider sharing. Ask for extra pickles. Take photos with the famous "Where Harry Met Sally" sign.
