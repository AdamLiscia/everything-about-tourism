Lark Street

Known as "Albany's Greenwich Village," Lark Street is the heart of the city's arts and entertainment district. This historic neighborhood blends Victorian architecture with modern culture, featuring independent shops, diverse restaurants, art galleries, and vibrant nightlife.

Neighborhood Highlights

Dining & Cafes

Explore diverse cuisine from casual cafes to fine dining establishments. Known for international flavors and local favorites.

Events & Activities

LarkFest

Annual fall street festival featuring live music, local vendors, food, and art. The Capital Region's largest one-day street festival.

Art on Lark

Summer arts festival showcasing regional artists, craftspeople, and performers.

First Friday

Monthly arts walk featuring gallery openings, special exhibitions, and performances throughout the neighborhood.

Visitor Information

Getting There

Located between Washington Park and downtown Albany. Street parking available. CDTA bus routes serve the area. Walking distance from Empire State Plaza.

Best Times to Visit

Weekday evenings and weekends are busiest. Summer for outdoor dining and events. First Fridays for gallery openings. Fall for LarkFest.

Local Tips

Many businesses are closed Mondays. Check event calendar for special programming. Consider exploring nearby Washington Park and Center Square.
