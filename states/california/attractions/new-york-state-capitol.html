New York State Capitol

The New York State Capitol, completed in 1899, is a masterpiece of 19th-century architecture blending Romanesque and Renaissance Revival styles. This National Historic Landmark took 32 years to complete at a cost of $25 million, making it one of the most expensive government buildings of its time.

Visitor Information

Hours & Access

Open Monday-Friday, 7AM-7PM. Photo ID required for entry. Security screening required. Closed major holidays.

Getting There

Located at State Street & Washington Avenue. Paid parking available at nearby garages. Walking distance from Empire State Plaza.

Visitor Tips

Arrive 15 minutes early for tours. Photography allowed without flash. Wear comfortable walking shoes. Visit the Hall of Governors.
