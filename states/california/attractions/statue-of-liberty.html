Statue of Liberty National Monument

Standing proudly in New York Harbor, the Statue of Liberty has welcomed millions to America since 1886. A gift from France, this iconic 305-foot copper statue represents freedom and democracy, featuring a museum, observation deck, and crown access for visitors.

Highlights

Museum

Interactive exhibits about the statue's construction, history, and symbolism. Original torch on display.

Ellis Island

Combined ticket includes access to Ellis Island Immigration Museum. Research family history.

Audio Tour

Free audio tour available in multiple languages. Covers history, architecture, and immigration stories.

Visitor Information

Ferry Service

Ferries depart from Battery Park (NYC) and Liberty State Park (NJ). Arrive 30 minutes early. Security screening required.

Best Times

First ferry or late afternoon for fewer crowds. Spring and fall for mild weather. Book crown access 3-6 months ahead.

What to Bring

Photo ID for crown access. Comfortable shoes. Water and snacks allowed. No large bags or luggage.
